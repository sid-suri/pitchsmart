Warm-Up Drills

Light movements designed to activate the shoulder and arm before throwing.

Why Warm-Ups Matter

A proper warm-up increases blood flow, improves mobility, and prepares the arm for throwing. Skipping this step increases injury risk, especially for youth pitchers.

These drills should feel easy. If anything hurts, stop.

Recommended Warm-Up Drills

Arm Circles

How: Small to large controlled circles, forward and backward.

Reps: 20 each direction

Focus: Smooth motion, no rushing.

Scapular Push-Ups

How: Keep arms straight, pinch shoulder blades together and apart.

Reps: 2 sets of 10

Focus: Shoulder blade control.

Wrist Flicks

How: Light ball or no ball, flick wrist forward.

Reps: 20 per arm

Focus: Loose wrist, relaxed grip.

Elbow Flex & Extend

How: Slowly bend and straighten the elbow.

Reps: 15 per arm

Focus: Full range of motion.

Torso Rotations

How: Rotate upper body side to side with arms relaxed.

Reps: 20 total

Focus: Smooth trunk movement.

When to Use These Drills

Perform this warm-up before:

  • Any throwing session
  • Games or bullpen work
  • Practices involving pitching
